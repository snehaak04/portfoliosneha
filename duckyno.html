Ducky, No!

Hybrid-casual Game

A pixel platformer game following a criminal duck named Quacksenberg who navigates through dungeons and worse as he dodges treacherous obstacles and outwits snarky skeletons while trying to escape his enemies.

The Process

The aim was to design an end-to-end hybrid-casual mobile game by basing it off of an already existing reference game. The game we chose was ‘Yeah Bunny!’ by Adrian Zarzycki.

The Differences

Yeah Bunny!

Ducky, No!

  • Theme : While Yeah Bunny! follows a cute and bright theme, our game goes in a darker route with a shady main character.
  • Physics : Based on the reviews complaining about the reference game’s unavoidable wall bounce function, the duck in our game sticks to the wall if he hits it and moves again once the player taps the screen.
  • Characters : Our game introduces other characters, mostly a skeleton named Boney who tells the duck how to play the game and other instructions. The duck also has to get keys in each level to unlock the door to a trapped character and finish the level.
  • Daily Puzzle : DUCKY NO! has a daily puzzle mini puzzle game with a hidden object search game: the player has to find an object that was used as an accessory to a crime that was committed in the room. Finding objects to get stamps every day gives the player the stamp of the crime on the last day of the week.
  • Leaderboard : Also not available in Yeah Bunny!, the leaderboard provides players with ranking based on their coin count and puts them in leagues based on their performance. They receive rewards based on their rank and league.
  • Skins : Not provided in Yeah Bunny, the game gives the players options to play in different skins that they unlock either by buying them with coins from the game, earning a high rank on the leaderboard or by paying with actual money. Value of skin varies based on their rarity.
  • Economy : Yeah Bunny! has the player get carrots from the game which is in turn spent on reviving the bunny after failing a level; it is the only form of economy they have. Our game uses the coins earned from the levels to revive, buy skins and potions from the store.
